Why is TLS important?
TLS encryption, or Transport Layer Security, is a way that companies like us can safeguard your information.
How it works
The way it works is much more complicated than its purpose. A website proves that it is an owner of a domain and legitimate with a TLS certificate, which is shown with the https:// in front of sites. TLS and the website then do a handshake to authenticate each other and agree upon a session key. Then, using that key, they transfer all the information they need to, and they use a Message Authentication Code (MAC) to ensure that no data has been tampered with.
